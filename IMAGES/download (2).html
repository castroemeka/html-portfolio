    All about the Leh Ladakh Tour 6 Days.

    Ladakh is situated at an elevation of 2750 – 7672 meters above sea level. This place is considered as one of the most explored trails in the Indian Himalayas and is known not only for its beautiful landscapes that range from subtropical plains to steep mountains and valleys but also for its monasteries. It has a culture that is very unique and different from that of other regions of the country.

    In this Leh Ladakh tour, you can discover the astonishing beauty while going through these roads and finding out more about the Buddhist culture practiced here. The expedition will run in the Great Himalayas, where the scenic beauty and a rich culture will make your trip unforgettable. On this trip, you get a chance to explore Nubra Valley, Pangong Tso, Magnetic Hill, Sangam Point and many more places. As you drive along its magnificent roads, one can feel the wind in their face as you immerse yourself in the magic of beautiful monasteries, exceptionally rich traditions of the destination. This trip to Leh Ladakh proves to be a heaven for travellers with its breath-taking valleys, ridges and quaint villages. So, if you haven’t been to this place then you should because there is no other way to enjoy to the fullest than to explore Leh Ladakh. So, this Leh Ladakh adventure tour provides an opportunity to explore and have a thrilling experience for a lifetime.

    1. Day 1 Arrival at Leh and Acclimatization.

      Arrive at Leh, then check in to the hotel that we have booked for you. You can take a rest for the whole day to adjust to the new climate and surroundings.

      Post lunch we will visit Leh Palace which is popularly known as ’Lache Palkhar’, Shanti Stupa which is situated at a height adjacent to Changspa. When you reach at the top, you will witness the mesmerizing views of the Leh town. You can shop from the Leh Bazaar where you will get each thing that is essential for the tour and for remembrance. Back to the hotel and overnight stay at Leh

       

    2. Day 2 Leh Sightseeing (124 Km / 5-6 hrs)

      Today after breakfast we will make our journey for sightseeing to Pathar Sahib Gurudwara, Hall of Fame Museum, Magnetic Hill and Sangam ( convergence of Zanskar and Indus River). Then we will return to Leh by evening and stay overnight.

    3. Day 3 Leh - Khardung La Pass - Nubra Valley (120 Kms/ 6-7 hrs)

      After breakfast, we will move to Nubra Valley which is appraised by the travellers for it’s beauty and splendid views in the entire Trans-Himalayan region. Moving forward, we will cross the Khardung Pass (18,380 ft.) and is known as the highest motorable pass in the entire world. For adventure enthusiasts, all over the world, this pass is a dream destination. This place will give you such happiness with the picturesque views that should be captured. After that we will drive towards Nubra Valley, moving towards the North Pullu check post and then through the strait and curvy roads to reach Diskit in the afternoon. We get a chance to explore the Diskit Monastery and the Maitreya Buddha Statue at the hillock in the valley where the sight looks amazing.

      As we move further, we will go to the Hunder Sand Dunes, which is one of the famous tourist attractions in Ladakh. Nubra is the only place in India where we can enjoy these Bactrian Camel safaris. We will stay overnight at Camp or Homestay at the Nubra.

    4. Day 4 Drive from Nubra - Pangong Tso - Via Shyok (190 Km / 7 hrs)

      So, now this day we will travel to the world’s popular Pangong Lake, which is undoubtedly the must-visit destination during your trip to Ladakh. It is the brackish lake in Asia and the largest lake at thisaltitude. The road from Nubra to Shyok is off the beaten path but the most fascinating views which will compel you to stop for a picture at every turn. You will get to see some water crossings and off-road stretches which is extremely exciting and lively. In the afternoon you can lunch at Durbuk and move to the Pangong Lake within the night underneath the sky and gazing at the twinkling stars Where you may positively live the moment. Stay overnight at the camp in the Pangong.

    5. Day 5 Pangong - Chang La Pass - Leh (180 Km/ 8 hrs)

      Wake up early so you will get a chance to see the mesmerizing sunrise on the lake. After you finish breakfast, move back to Leh where on the way you can spend time at Thiksey Monastery and Shey Palace. In the evening get back to Leh. Also, we will cross the Changla Pass, the 2nd Highest motorable pass. The region is a paradise, as it has water streams from the glacier which makes it even more fun. Then, We will go down to the Changla Pass and drive to Leh. Stay Overnight at Leh.

       

    6. Day 6 Departure from Leh

      Today morning after breakfast, we will leave the hotel for the airport. An unforgettable Leh Ladakh adventure journey ends here with lots of memories.

    We can reach Ladakh by the following three ways-

    • The best way to is to travel by taking a flight. Leh Airport is well associated with the major cities including Delhi, Chandigarh, Jammu, Srinagar and many more urban cities of India.
    • In case you are planning to travel by road then there are two routes to reach Ladakh. One is from Srinagar and another one is from Manali.
    • Travelling from Manali will lead you to cover a distance of 474 km to Leh by way of Keylong along with a night drop-off.
    • Travelling from Srinagar to Leh will make you cross Sonamarg, Drass, Kargil, Mulbekh and Lamayuru and cover a distance of 434 km. The roads are open from May to October.

    The summer season May to September is the ideal time to visit Ladakh because the weather remains pleasant when the snow melts. The environment all around looks beautiful and worth seeing.

    There are numerous accommodation facilities available in Ladakh. There are various guest houses, hotels, camps, homestays. You don’t have to face many difficulties finding the perfect accommodation as per your own wish.

    The weather in Ladakh is cold, so you have to prepare yourself to survive in this chilly climate. You need to bring Woolen clothes that keep you warm including woollen gloves, sweater, windproof jackets etc.

    It is important for everyone to bring documents so that you won’t face any kind of problem during your journey.

    Documents included:

    • Voter Id Card
    • Passport
    • Aadhar Card
    • Driving Licence

    You will only find ATM facility in Leh, Manali and Srinagar. It is advisable for you to carry cash along your way.

    Yes, Credit Cards work in hotels and restaurants in Ladakh but it includes 2% service tax.

    There is nothing to worry about this because there is a large number of female travellers who travel alone. Also in case of emergency, there are local teams for you.
